# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Wedding guests opening a personal invitation link, usually on a phone, often while moving between WhatsApp, Instagram, calendar apps, maps, and family conversations. They need to feel welcomed, understand the event quickly, RSVP without friction, and find the venue without calling the couple or planner.

The secondary users are the couple, family representatives, and planners who need accurate RSVP data, fewer repeated questions, reliable guest communication, and a keepsake that can transform after the wedding.

## Product Purpose

Create a premium digital wedding experience platform that replaces a static invitation card with an emotional, interactive, mobile-first journey. It should excite guests, make attendance easy, collect RSVP intent, support directions and reminders, and eventually become a post-wedding memory hub.

Success means guests immediately understand whose wedding it is, feel personally invited, can RSVP and navigate with confidence, and remember the invitation as something worth talking about.

## Brand Personality

Luxurious, romantic, and playful. The experience should feel intimate and crafted, with moments of delight such as opening, scratching, revealing, following-cursor characters, cinematic transitions, and soft celebratory motion. It should feel premium without becoming stiff.

## Anti-references

This should not feel like a plain wedding website, a generic template landing page, a desktop-first brochure, or a beige AI-generated card grid. Avoid lifeless section stacking, generic gradient text, overused glass panels, repetitive icon cards, and animations that slow down core actions.

The invitation should not hide important logistics behind decoration. The premium feel must support RSVP, schedule, map, contacts, and updates.

## Design Principles

1. Phone-first ceremony: design every primary moment for one-handed mobile use before scaling up to laptop.
2. Reveal, do not dump: unfold details through opening, scratch, scroll, and staged transitions so the invitation feels alive.
3. Emotion before administration: lead with the couple, date, story, and atmosphere, then make logistics effortless.
4. Personal by default: unique guest welcomes, RSVP state, and reminders should make each guest feel expected.
5. Beauty must reduce friction: maps, program, contacts, and RSVP must be more accessible because of the design, not buried by it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support reduced motion preferences with simplified reveals. Keep RSVP, maps, schedule, and contact actions usable without relying on animation alone. Use clear labels, generous tap targets, readable mobile type, and avoid color-only status cues.
